CODATA-RDA Schools of Research Data Science

I am a co-chair and instructor of the CODATA-RDA Schools of Research Data Science (RDS). A strategic priority shared by CODATA and the Research Data Alliance (RDA) is to build capacity and develop skills, training young researchers in the principles of RDS to address transdisciplinary research challenges, particularly in low- and middle-income countries. My role is to help strategise and organise schools across the world and teach modules at these schools.

The Carpentries

The Carpentries teaches foundational coding and data science skills to researchers worldwide to build global capacity to allow researchers to conduct efficient, open, and reproducible research. I have been a qualified instructor for The Carpentries since 2016 and became a certified intructor trainer in 2020. I have taught at multiple workshops over the past few years, which provided me with the opportunity to address the shortage of data science skills and also train new instructors in pedagogy so that they can run more regional workshops in the future, thus making these workshops more sustainable while ensuring quality teaching.

Research Data Alliance: CODATA-RDA Research Data Science Schools for Low and Middle Income Countries Interest Group

I am a chair of the Research Data Alliance: CODATA-RDA Research Data Science Schools for Low and Middle Income Countries Interest Group. The goals of this group are to continue the information sharing, targeted outreach and community collaboration with RDA members about the CODATA-RDA Schools for Research Data Science. This is aligned with the RDA mission in that it enables data sharing through its training and adds value to the RDA community by teaching some of the outputs of the RDA in Research Data Management.

Projects

During my postgraduate studies I conducted genetic studies of Busseola fusca (Lepidoptera: Noctuidae), a major insect pest in sub-Saharan Africa. This pest has developed high levels of non-recessive resistance to the Cry1Ab toxin expressed in Bt maize within eight years after the initial cultivation. I performed statistical and network analyses of mitochondrial gene sequences to determine whether the development and spread of resistant populations of this pest would be a potential threat. Additionally, we performed a de novo transcriptome assembly and conducted differential gene expression ((DE) analysis. The DE results suggested that differential expression of metabolic and immune-related genes might explain resistance to the Cry1Ab toxin in this pest. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) projects: (1) Biological crusts of serpentine and non-serpentine soils from the Barberton Greenstone Belt of South Africa. The aim of this study was to compare the cryptogamic composition of serpentine and non-serpentine soils by using integrative taxonomy, which combines morphological and DNA barcoding data, to determine how soil characteristics in combination with rainfall can influence BSC community composition. (2) NGS analysis to determine the contribution of forbs to the diet of elephant, impala and cattle in the dry season of a semi-arid savanna (Kruger National Park, South Africa) (publication in preparation).

ExAMIN Youth SA study

I am currently managing the follow-up ExAMIN Youth SA study of the Hypertension in Africa Research Team (HART) of the North-West University. This study aims to investigate the impact of lifestyle behaviours (physical fitness/activity, dietary intake and psychosocial* factors) involved in early vascular aging among South African children.
